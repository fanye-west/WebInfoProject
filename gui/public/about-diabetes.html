ABOUT DIABETES

What is diabetes?

Diabetes is a health condition which affects 4.9% of the population. Diabetes is an inability to regulate glucose levels in the body through the use of the hormone insulin. Diabetes is a serious condition which requires daily self care in order to maintain a healthy and enjoyable life.

Types of diabetes

There are 3 types of diabetes. Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune disease in which a persons immune system destroys the cells in the pancreas which produce insulin. This type of diabetes does not develop from modifiable lifestyle factors. Type 2 diabetes is a condition where the body becomes resistant to the normal effects of insulin loses the ability to produce enough insulin. This type of diabetes has a strong genetic factor and is often also associated with modifiable lifestyle factors, some people are able to slow or halt this condition by changing aspects of their lives. The final type of diabetes is gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) and develops during pregnancy, generally this diabetes stops after pregnancy.

Managing Diabetes

Diabetes is most commonly controlled through diet, exercise, medication, insulin therapy and regular monitoring. Despite being a scary diseases there has now been a lot of research into diabetes and healthcare professionals work closely with people to support healthy lives.
